Product Branding
An new make-up company asked me to create the photography work for their new website. Obviously, having high-quality and meaningful imagery is essential for success. But it's more than just showing off your your product on models with beauty shots. Images need implicitly communicate the market you're trying to reach. What is the lifestyle associated with these products, and how does that lifestyle apply to me?

Planning and Research
The shoot start with a vision. Questions are asked, such as 'why are we doing this?", and "what do we want to accomplish?". Brainstorm documents and phone calls helped the founders explain what they wanted, so I can figure out what they need. I also dug to learn about the company's mission and values: universal concepts which should appear in every aspect of the company, including their content.

Meanwhile, I began researching the other players in the industry. What do the websites of other make-up companies look like? This allowed me to get a better understanding of how images are being used to connect with their consumers. Specifically, at what target market is this image aimed, and what qualitative and quanitative image features were used to communicate that market directioning?

Population Statistics
Infographics are a great tool for helping people better understand information. I am currently experimenting with using infographics to communicate co-op employment statistics for our class. My goal is to better understand how specific visualization techniques affect one's interpretation of ambiguous data.
